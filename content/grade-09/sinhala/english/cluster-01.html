Everybody is Good at Something

Everyone is talented at something! Grammar: prepositions · Vocabulary: collective & compound nouns.

By the end of this unit you will be able to 🎯
  • use prepositions of place, time and "good at"
  • use collective nouns (with the right verb) and form compound nouns
  • read a talent-show dialogue, write descriptions, and act out conversations
1a Grammar: Prepositions ›
1 Hook
When you tell a friend where the kade is — "next to the bank, opposite the temple, at the corner" — those little linking words are prepositions. Tiny words, but they decide the whole meaning!
2 Plain explanation

A preposition shows the place, time or direction of something. It comes before a noun or pronoun.

TypeWordsExample
Placein, on, at, under, behind, next to, opposite, betweenThe bag is under the desk.
Timeat (time), on (day), in (month/year)at 5 o'clock · on Monday · in April
Talentgood at + (activity)She is good at singing.
We celebrate Avurudu in April, on the 14th, at the auspicious time.
Remember: AT a time, ON a day, IN a month/year (smallest → largest).
5 Watch out!
⚠ "good at", not "good in"
For a talent always say good at (good at maths, good at cricket). And since = from a point in time (since 2015); for = a length of time (for two years).
✏️ Your turn: "The match starts ___ 3 p.m. ___ Sunday."
Show answer
starts at 3 p.m. on Sunday. (time → at; day → on)
🔑
AT a time · ON a day · IN a month. Talent = good AT something.
1b Vocabulary: Collective & compound nouns ›
1 Hook
A group of cricketers playing together is a team. One word for a whole group — that's a collective noun.
2 Collective nouns (Grade 9 textbook, Unit 1)
armyaudiencebandboardcommitteecrewfamilyjuryorchestrapublicstaffteam
Singular or plural verb? Use a singular verb if the group acts as one unit; a plural verb if the members act individually.
There was quite a large audience. (the audience as one unit)
The audience were already clapping their hands. (members acting individually)
3 Compound nouns

Two words joined to make one new noun.

foot + ball = footballblack + board = blackboardrain + bow = rainbowtooth + brush = toothbrush
🎮 Collective & compound nouns — choose the answer
5 Watch out!
⚠ Match the verb to the meaning
"The team is strong" (one unit) vs "The team are wearing their new jerseys" (each player). Decide: one unit or individuals?
🔑
Collective = one word for a group; verb = singular if "as one", plural if "as members". Compound = two words → one new noun.

The Greatest Wealth

Health is the greatest wealth. Grammar: conditional clauses (Type 1 & 2) · Vocabulary: affixes, synonyms & antonyms.

By the end of this unit you will be able to 🎯
  • build Type 1 (real) and Type 2 (unreal) conditional sentences
  • form words with prefixes & suffixes; give synonyms & antonyms
  • read folk tales, write instructions, and handle telephone conversations
9a Grammar: Conditional clauses — Type 1 & Type 2 ›
1 Hook
Two friends dream: "If I won the Mahajana Sampatha lottery, I would build a house!" The other: "If I won, I would just stay healthy and happy." Both are conditionals — sentences that start with if. The big question: real or just a dream?
2 The key difference

Type 1 — real / likely

Something that can really happen. The result happens only if the condition is met.

If + present, … will + verb

Type 2 — unreal / unlikely

Imaginary or contrary to fact.

If + past, … would + verb
4 Examples (Grade 9 textbook, Unit 9)
Type 1: If I go to the pharmacy, I will buy a roll of bandage.
If-clause first → use a comma. (No comma if the main clause comes first.)
Type 1: If it rains today, they will call off the match. 🏏☔
Type 2: If I knew his phone number, I would give him a call.
Fact: I don't know it → that's why it's Type 2. Past tense, but the meaning is present/future — NOT past.
🎮 Complete the conditional — choose the correct verb
5 Watch out!
⚠ Don't put "will" in the if-clause
Type 1 is "If it rains…" — NOT "If it will rain…". And in Type 2, the past tense (knew/saw) means present/future, not the past.
✏️ Your turn: "If you eat too much sugar, you ___ (get) sick." (Type 1)
Show answer
you will get sick. (Type 1: if + present → will + verb)
🔑
Type 1 = real (present → will). Type 2 = dream (past → would). "would" = a dream!"
9b Vocabulary: Affixes · Synonyms & Antonyms ›
2 Affixes (prefixes & suffixes)

An affix is added to the start or end of a word to make a new word. (e.g. healthy → unhealthy, polite → impolite, neat → neatness)

Prefix (at the start)Suffix (at the end)
Effectchanges the meaningchanges the word class
e.g.un-, dis-, im-, ir-, in-, mis-, re--ful, -less, -ness, -ment, -tion, -able, -ly
samplehappy → unhappy · advantage → disadvantageuse → useless · agree → agreement
forget (verb) → forgetful (adjective) · happy (adj) → happiness (noun) · angry (adj) → angrily (adverb)
A suffix changes the word's "job" (its word class).
3 Synonyms & Antonyms

Synonyms (same meaning)

big = large · happy = glad · begin = start · clever = smart

Antonyms (opposite)

healthy ↔ unhealthy · increase ↔ decrease · win ↔ lose

✏️ Your turn: "She answered with great ___ (confident)."
Show answer
confidence (a noun is needed → confident → confidence)
⚠ Prefix vs suffix
Prefix changes meaning (un-/dis- = "not" → opposites). Suffix changes word class (-ly → adverb, -ness → noun).
🔑
Prefix = meaning; Suffix = word class. Synonym = same; Antonym = opposite.

Be Happy, Be Bright, Be You!

Live positively, be yourself. Grammar: reported speech · Vocabulary: word classes.

By the end of this unit you will be able to 🎯
  • turn direct speech into reported speech (with the right changes)
  • identify word classes: noun, verb, adjective, adverb
  • read poems, infer meaning, and write for official purposes
10a Grammar: Reported speech ›
1 Hook
Reported speech is basically how you pass on a message (or share a bit of gossip 😄). Your brother said "I am hungry." You tell amma: "Malli said he was hungry." Notice — "am" became "was"!
2 The changes (Grade 9 textbook, Unit 10)

When we report what someone said, we don't use their exact words. Three things change:

1. Tense shifts back2. Time / place words
present → past (like → liked)today → that day
past → past perfect (went → had gone)tomorrow → the next day
present perfect → past perfecthere → there · this → that · now → then

(3. Pronouns change too: I → he/she, my → his/her …)

Direct: "I like English Literature."
Reported: She said that she liked English Literature.
By sentence type: Statement → say/tell + that ("…that she liked…"). Imperative (command) → told + (someone) + to + verb: "Amali, do Art." → She told Amali to do Art.
🎮 Reported speech — choose the correct version
5 Watch out!
⚠ Don't forget all three changes
(1) shift the tense back, (2) change pronouns, (3) change today→that day, here→there. Many students change the tense but forget the time words!
✏️ Your turn: She said, "I will come tomorrow." → reported?
Show answer
She said that she would come the next day. (will→would; tomorrow→the next day)
🔑
Reported = tense back + pronouns change + today→that day. say + that; tell + (sb) + to.
10b Vocabulary: Word classes ›
2 Every word has a "job"
ClassJobExamples
Nounnames a person/thing/placeteacher, Colombo, happiness
Verban action or staterun, sing, is, become
Adjectivedescribes a nounhappy, tall, bright
Adverbdescribes a verbquickly, happily, well
happy (adjective) → happiness (noun) → happily (adverb)
Just like affixes — a suffix changes the word class! (-ly is usually an adverb.)
⚠ Spot the job in the sentence
Look at what the word does. -ly → usually adverb; -ness/-ment/-tion → noun; -ful/-less → adjective.
🔑
Noun=name, Verb=action, Adjective=describes noun, Adverb=describes verb.

Putting it all together — your grammar map 🧭

These three units gave you three big grammar tools, each a step up in difficulty:

Prepositions→Conditionals (Type 1 & 2)→Reported speech

• Prepositions (Unit 1) — the small linking words: AT a time, ON a day, IN a month; good AT something.
• Conditionals (Unit 9) — Type 1 real (present → will), Type 2 unreal (past → would).
• Reported speech (Unit 10) — tense back + pronouns + today→that day.
• Vocabulary thread: affixes & word classes connect Units 9 and 10 — a suffix changes a word's class.

Unit 10
  1. Reported: Nimal said, "I am tired."
    Answer
    Nimal said that he was tired. (am→was, I→he)
