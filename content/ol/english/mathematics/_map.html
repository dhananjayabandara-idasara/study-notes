O/L Maths — The Whole Map

Maths isn't a pile of topics. It's a chain — each step stands on the one before it. Tuition teaches the steps out of order, one a week. This map shows the order they actually depend on, grouped the way the exam tests them. Follow the chain. Come and see for yourself.

Read this first — the exam maths nobody tells you. The paper is split in two, each worth half. Paper 1 is short questions on the Essential Learning Concepts (marked below as P1). Paper 2 is longer essay questions — Part A is Algebra, Part B is Geometry. Here's the part that changes everything: score 70% on Paper 1 alone and you pass Maths — even with zero on Paper 2. So if you're fighting for a pass, the green P1 topics are where every hour pays back first.
